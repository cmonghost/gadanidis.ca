Broad vs. narrow transcription

Broad transcription: Surrounded by slashes; shows individual segments without much detail.

Narrow transcription: Surrounded by square brackets; shows individual segments with as much detail as possible.

The textbook uses square brackets for everything, but we are going to use both.

Aspiration

Stops at the beginning of a word are aspirated. Not shown in broad transcription; represented by a superscript h, e.g. [tʰæp], in narrow transcription.

Nasalization

Vowels adjacent to a nasal segment are nasalized. Not shown in broad transcription; represented by a superscript tilde, e.g. [flãn], in narrow transcription.

Canadian Raising

The diphthongs /aj/ and /aw/ are raised to [ʌj] and [ʌw] before a voiceless stop. Not shown in broad transcription; shown in narrow transcription. e.g. /tajt/ vs [tʌjt].
